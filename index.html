Section 02 · Visual Findings

Five charts. One thesis.

Each chart below is rendered in the official New York Knicks palette by src/visualize.py. They're the artifacts a coaching staff actually reads — code-free, glanceable, decision-grade.

Chart 01 · Series Box

The shape of a sweep.

Margins widened across the series. The Game 4 closeout was the largest scoring differential of the four games and the most lopsided ECF closeout for New York in three decades.

  • Avg margin: +18.75 points across four games
  • Trend: margin grew Game 1 → Game 4 (+11 → +37)
  • Read: Cleveland did not adjust between games
Chart 02 · Lineup Net Rating

The bench mob was the best unit on the floor.

Net rating is offensive rating minus defensive rating, possession-weighted across all four ECF games. The BENCH_MOB grouping outpaced the starters and the closing five.

  • BENCH_MOB (+8.88): McBride, Alvarado, Anunoby, Shamet, Robinson — 88 possessions
  • STARTERS (+6.28): Brunson, Bridges, Anunoby, Hart, Towns — 92 possessions
  • Read: the second unit was a tempo and switchability advantage Cleveland couldn't match
Chart 03 · Scheme Impact

ICE > DROP > SWITCH. By a lot.

Aggregated across every lineup-game observation, the gap between Cleveland's worst-handled coverage (ICE) and best-handled (SWITCH) was the single largest swing factor in the series.

  • ICE: +4.90 net per 100 — the look Cleveland never solved
  • DROP: +1.24 net — viable, situational
  • SWITCH: -2.87 net — Cleveland punished it. Reduce exposure.
  • Swing: 7.77 points per 100 between best and worst scheme
Chart 04 · Player Efficiency

Brunson and Towns carried the usage. Shamet was the efficiency surprise.

True Shooting blends 2P, 3P, and free-throw scoring efficiency. Usage Rate is the share of team possessions a player finished. The Knicks closed the series with three players above 60% TS.

  • Brunson: 63.55% TS / 23.63% USG — ECF MVP
  • Towns: 65.02% TS / 22.6% USG
  • Shamet: 91.51% TS on lower volume — a scalable role-player profile
Chart 05 · Data Quality

Four anomalies were caught upstream. None reached the metrics.

Every record is run against schema, type, and plausibility rules before it touches the analytics. Bad rows are quarantined and logged with a reason. The pipeline always tells the consumer what was excluded and why.

  • NYK_15: fgm (18) exceeds fga (4) — impossible shooting line
  • NYK_03: missing required field minutes_played
  • N/A: player_id was null
  • NYK_02: minutes_played out of range (-7.2)

Section 06 · Finals Carry-Overs

Three recommendations for the next round.

Distilled from the lineup, scheme, and data-quality findings above. These are the items the brief tells the head coach to take forward into Finals prep.

01 / Lineup

Open the Finals with BENCH_MOB minutes early.

The +8.88 net rating against Cleveland is the most reliable scoreboard signal in the dataset. Treat it as the default situational group until the opponent solves it.

02 / Scheme

Base coverage = ICE. Hold SWITCH in reserve.

The 7.77-per-100 swing between ICE and SWITCH is the single largest tactical lever in the series. Default to ICE; deploy SWITCH only situationally late in games.

03 / Process

Run the guardrail every game-day.

The Finals will produce a different upstream feed than the ECF. Any new schema break gets quarantined before it touches the analytics. The pipeline keeps producing trustworthy numbers when the feed breaks.

Section 07 · Pipeline Architecture

How the brief got built.

Five stages. Stdlib-first Python. Zero dependencies for the pipeline itself; matplotlib is only used to render the PNGs. Runs anywhere Python 3.9+ is installed.

Stage 01

Ingestion

Generates per-game player and lineup JSON. Intentionally injects 4 upstream anomalies to simulate feed corruption.

src/ingestion.py
Stage 02

Validation

A strict Data Contract layer. Every record is checked against schema, type, and plausibility rules.

src/validation.py
Stage 03

Analytics

TS%, USG%, possession-weighted Off / Def / Net Ratings per lineup, and tactical scheme impact.

src/analytics.py
Stage 04

Reporter

Emits a coach-facing markdown brief. Code-free output. Consumable by staff with zero engineering literacy.

src/reporter.py
Stage 05

Visualizer

Renders 5 portfolio-grade PNGs in the official Knicks palette — Blue, Orange, Silver, Black.

src/visualize.py
Section 08 · SQL Layer

Same metrics, expressed in PostgreSQL.

The Python pipeline is the production path. The SQL mirror in sql/ proves the metric definitions are portable to any data warehouse — Snowflake, BigQuery, Redshift, Postgres — without rewriting them. Coaches, GMs, and analysts read SQL faster than Python.

sql/01_lineup_net_rating.sql PostgreSQL 16

What this query does: Computes possession-weighted Net Rating (Off Rtg − Def Rtg) for every 5-man lineup across all four ECF games, then ranks them. The output matches src/analytics.py exactly — that's the contract.
